  • WIFI/Bluetooth: A WIFI is a wireless transmitter that recieves information from a network via is connection. It converts the recived inforation into radio waves then transmitts them. A device receives radio waves through installed wireless adapters which allow it to download information from a data source. Bluetooth sends and receives radio waves in a band of 79 different frequencies. These frrequencies are all centrered around 2.45Ghz. Devices using bluetooth automatically find and connect to eachother, but they don't interfere with other devices as they are on different frequencies and channels
  • LANS: There are two types of LANS WLANS and LANS. WANS are used where computers or networks are situated a long distance away from eachother. A number of LANS joined together can form a WAN. The most common types of WAN include the internet and the network of ATMS used by banks. WLANS are similar to WANS but there are no wires or cables involved. They provide wireless comunications over short distances using raios and infrared signals instead of cables.
